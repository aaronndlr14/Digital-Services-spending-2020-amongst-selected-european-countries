In this small project I was hoping to mainly just sharpen my Juypyter notebook skillset with some practice.
The data set that was used is from the Irish Central statistics office. https://data.cso.ie/ and the data set code is OSS02
The data set is Household Expenditure on Digital Services for the year 2020 and looks at seven european nations, ( France, Germany, Ireland, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands and United Kingdom.)


The tools that I used were Juypter Notebook, Pandas and Plotly,

it is worth noting that a lot of the data was missing at country level. not every country had figures for every service category, so some charts only show the countries that actually had data.

--key findings 

An interesting finding for Ireland is its dominance in App Store spending (€94m, 67.6% of its total digital spend). This is particularly interesting in the Irish context, where illegal streaming devices commonly known as 'dodgy boxes'  are widely used to access TV and sports content for free,which is suppressing Video Streaming figures. Despite this, Irish households remain heavy App Store spenders, raising questions about what exactly is driving that expenditure.

The UK  dominates online gambling spending at €95m which is miles ahead of every other country in the dataset. This should not come as surprise as it is well known gambling is very popular in the United Kingdom. Ontop of that they also have a very high number for video streaming. Now it is important to know if that would include sports streaming and if that is the case, the data makes sense as it shows the Uk as a nation of people that like to watch things and gamble on it.
